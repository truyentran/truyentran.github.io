Research areas

The Centre for Pattern Recognition and Data Analytics (PRaDA), led by Professor Svetha Venkatesh, is a multidisciplinary research body, currently undertaking research in the following areas:

Machine learning
  • Deep learning: a key to machine learning is to effectively and efefciently represent raw complex data in a manner that offers higher level of inderstanding and makes the data processing tasks easier. The goal of representation learning is to automatically extract useful representations of data. Deep learning offers a layer-by-layer way to achieve that goal. This is not only efficient but also offers wider coverage than the traditional manual feature engineering. This enables end-to-end learning with minimal human supervision.
  • Nonparametric Bayesian methods: addressing the limits of current parametric models by letting the complexity of the models grow with data and automatically determine the model size that fits the data.
  • Lean data: current machine learning is data hungry. There are many areas that do not have much data, for example, experiments in manufacturing. The goal is to learn useful things that are data efficient.

Applications

  • Healthcare analytics: modern health information systems have collected a huge amount of data which is mostly sitting there unused. By using advanced machine learning techniques, we aim at discovering useful clinical patterns, associations and causal rules as well as acurately predicting the outcomes a head of time. PRaDA has made huge progress in this area.
  • Pervasive/wearable/ubiquitous computing: leveraging our expertise in sensing, pattern recognitions and data modelling, we aim at developing technologies that can help improve lifestyle, work and health, assist decisions and and provide personalized medicines. See TOBY PlayPad for a product for autistic children.
  • Social media: continuing its strength in multimedia, PRaDA now extends its reach to social media, including blogs, social networks, personal/social media management. Due to its multimedia nature, research will deal with text, hyperlinks, image, audio, video and streaming data. Applications in sentiment analysis, mental health surveillance and public health measurement and tracking.
  • Other mahine learning application areas: recommender systems, manufacturing, software engineering, NLP & vision.

Selection criteria

There is no single selection criterion and we can only say that we look for candidates who have good potential to complete a PhD program and to have a successful research career, not just a PhD degree. If you do not know what it takes for a research career, I recommend that you should spend time to read about it. Sometimes it will help you decide whether you need a PhD or not. Here is the list of things we are looking for

  • Thirst of knowledge and willing to pursue a research career

  • Research experience (e.g. has published papers)

  • Academic recognition: university prizes and awards. High quality honours at high school (such as members of national or international teams) may also count. 

  • Bachelor/Master in Computer Science/Informatics/Information Technology with excellent academic performance from a well-recognised university. For those with Australian degrees, the standard requirement is an Honours degree. 

  • Good math background (we will use a lot of math for data modelling, likely probability/statistics and linear algebra)

  • Good programming skills

  • Good English (generally IELTS score 6.5 (with no bands below 6) or better is required for entry, but you should be aware that this score is not generally enough for smooth communication. Good reading skills with critical thinking are vital because you will spend up to 50% of your time for reading. Technical writing is also important because it is often the end-product for assessment.)

What to send

The rule of thumb is that you should send whatever (electronic) documents you think that will support your future research career. Before you send these, I recommend that you spend time to study research areas and publications by PRaDA. Documents are written in English and may include some of the following

  • A comprehensive resume (or CV)

  • Academic transcripts

  • Theses

  • Published papers or technical reports (if written in languages other than English, titles and abstracts should be translated to English)

  • A statement of purpose briefly describes your research intention and interest, general approach to reach your PhD/career goals

  • Any supporting documents, like reference letters from your professors

You can send your documents directly to Dr Santu Rana.
